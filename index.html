Video-Guided Universal Audio Generation with Mixture-Of-Expert Transformer at Scale

Paper Appendix

Anonymous Authors

Abstract. We propose a model with mixture of experts to scale video-to-audio generation. To tackle the challenges of modeling long audio signals, we propose flow-matching transformers to approximate the velocity vector field, which obtain data samples in the probability flow ordinary differential equation (ODE). To scale in terms of model capability, we stack mixture-of-experts (MoE) with both frequency-MoE and time-MoE layers, respectively for sounding semantics in specific audio frequency regions or denoising timesteps. Together with the power of contrastive video-audio pretraining (CVAP) and high-fidelity semantic compression, we achieve a deep level of video understanding with synchronized and high-fidelity generation. It even generalizes well to open-domain data (e.g., dancing video, or music video), or in multiple user-defined visual-guided audio generation tasks (e.g., continuation and interpolation), which empowers humans to create rich and diverse audio content with personalized transfer and fine-grained control.
